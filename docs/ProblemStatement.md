# Kinora Problem Statement

## Background

Families planning important life events often rely on multiple disconnected tools such as WhatsApp chats, spreadsheets, notebooks, phone contacts, screenshots, and paper checklists. Information becomes scattered across different people and different platforms.

## Problems

### 1. Information is scattered

Important information such as guest lists, vendor contacts, quotations, purchase lists, payments, and pending tasks are stored in different places, making it difficult to find the latest information when needed.

### 2. Lack of visibility

Family members do not always know the current status of tasks or responsibilities. This results in repeated questions, duplicated work, and unnecessary follow-ups.

### 3. Tracking becomes difficult

As the event grows, tracking guest counts, purchases, vendor payments, and pending activities becomes increasingly difficult and error-prone.

### 4. No single source of truth

Different people maintain their own notes or lists, leading to inconsistencies and confusion about which information is correct or up to date.

### 5. Planning becomes stressful

Managing multiple moving parts across different communication channels increases stress and consumes valuable time that could otherwise be spent on planning the event itself.

## Opportunity

There is an opportunity to simplify family coordination by providing one mobile-first platform where everyone can access the same accurate information, collaborate effectively, and track progress in real time.
