# Kinora – Product Discovery Decisions

**Version:** 0.1

**Status:** Approved

**Last Updated:** 30 June 2026

---

## Q1. Who creates a family?

A user creates a family and becomes the **Family Owner**.

The Family Owner can invite other members to join the family.

---

## Q2. Can a user belong to multiple families?

Yes.

A user may belong to multiple families. For example, after marriage, a person may participate in both their parents' family and their spouse's family.

---

## Q3. Can a family have multiple events?

Yes.

A family can create and manage multiple events over time, such as weddings, birthdays, anniversaries, housewarmings, and other family occasions.

---

## Q4. Can an event have multiple organizers?

Yes.

Each event has one **Event Owner**, who can assign additional **Event Organizers** to help coordinate planning and execution.

---

## Q5. Should every family member have access to all event information?

For the MVP, yes.

All family members can view event information and update tasks assigned to them.

Fine-grained permissions may be introduced in future releases if required.

---

## Q6. Can a task be assigned to multiple people?

Yes.

A task may have one or more assignees so that responsibilities can be shared.

---

## Q7. How should guests be organized?

Guests belong to a **Guest List**, not directly to an individual.

Examples of guest lists include:

* Bride's Family
* Groom's Family
* Friends
* Office
* Neighbours

This provides a flexible way to organize and manage invitations.

---

## Q8. Should vendors be reusable?

Yes.

Vendor information is maintained at the family level and can be reused across multiple events.

---

## Q9. What happens after an event is completed?

Completed events are archived rather than deleted.

Archived events remain available for future reference, including guest lists, vendor details, payments, and tasks.

---

## Q10. What should users see first?

The home screen should provide a clear overview of the family's current activities, including:

* Upcoming events
* Pending tasks
* Guest summary
* Vendor summary
* Recent activity

The dashboard should prioritize actionable information and help users quickly understand what requires attention.
